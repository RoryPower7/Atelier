Verbs, in five minutes.

Overview

— 01 · Le présent

Present tense.

Foundational 3 groups

How to form it: take the infinitive, drop the ending (-er, -ir, -re), and add the endings shown above. Used for things happening now, habits, and general truths.

The rule, in one line
  1. Find the stem: drop -er, -ir, or -re from the infinitive.
  2. Add the ending that matches the subject pronoun.
  3. For -ir verbs, don't forget the -iss- infix in the plural forms.

— 03 · L'impératif

The imperative.

Useful 3 forms only

Used to give commands, instructions, or invitations. There are only three forms — tu, nous, vous — and they're just the present tense without the pronoun. Common in messages, formal letters and advice-giving.

How to form it
  1. Take the present tense form for tu, nous, or vous.
  2. Drop the subject pronoun.
  3. For -er verbs, drop the final 's' from the tu form. tu parles → parle!

— 04 · Les verbes pronominaux

Reflexive verbs.

Trip-up zone être in past

Reflexive verbs describe actions you do to yourself: se laver (to wash oneself), se lever (to get up), s'amuser (to enjoy oneself). They take a reflexive pronoun (me, te, se, nous, vous, se) that matches the subject.

Two things to remember
  1. The pronoun goes before the verb: je me lève, nous nous levons.
  2. In the past tense, reflexive verbs always use être as the auxiliary, and the past participle agrees with the subject. elle s'est levée

— 05 · Le passé composé

Passé composé.

Essential Used constantly

The main past tense for completed actions: "I went, I did, I saw". Formed with the present tense of avoir or être, plus the past participle.

The formula
  1. Auxiliary verb (avoir or être, in present) + past participle.
  2. Most verbs take avoir: j'ai mangé, tu as fini, nous avons vendu.
  3. The "Dr & Mrs Vandertramp" verbs and all reflexives take être — and agree with the subject in number and gender.

vousêtes allé(e)(s)
ils/ellessont allé(e)s
Past participle endings
  1. -er verbs → -é (parler → parlé)
  2. -ir verbs → -i (finir → fini)
  3. -re verbs → -u (vendre → vendu)
  4. Irregulars: avoir → eu, être → été, faire → fait, voir → vu, prendre → pris, mettre → mis, écrire → écrit, dire → dit, vouloir → voulu, pouvoir → pu
In context
  • Hier, j'ai mangé au restaurant avec mes amis.Yesterday I ate at a restaurant with my friends.
  • Elle est partie à six heures.She left at six o'clock.
  • Nous avons vu un excellent film.We saw an excellent film.
— 06 · L'imparfait

The imperfect.

Essential One rule, no exceptions

Used for descriptions in the past, habits, and ongoing actions: "I used to, I was doing". Pairs beautifully with the passé composé to set scenes ("I was reading when he arrived"). Examiners love seeing the contrast.

Endings — same for every verb
  1. Take the nous form of the present (e.g. nous parlons), drop the -ons to get the stem.
  2. Add: -ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient.
  3. One exception: être uses the stem ét-.

  • Quand j'étais jeune, je jouais au foot tous les jours.When I was young, I used to play football every day.
  • Il faisait beau, alors nous sommes sortis.The weather was nice, so we went out.
— 07 · Le plus-que-parfait

The pluperfect.

Bonus marks "Had done"

Used for an action that happened before another past action: "I had already eaten when she arrived". A reliable way to show sophistication on the higher paper — use it once or twice per piece.

The formula
  1. Take the imparfait of avoir or être.
  2. Add the past participle, with same agreement rules as passé composé.

In context
  • Quand elle est arrivée, j'avais déjà mangé.When she arrived, I had already eaten.
  • Nous étions partis avant le début du film.We had left before the start of the film.
— 08 · Le futur simple

The future.

Essential Built on infinitive

Used for what will happen: "I will go, I will do". The stem for most verbs is the infinitive itself — making this one of the easier tenses to form.

Endings — same for every verb
  1. Stem: the infinitive (for -re verbs, drop the final e).
  2. Endings: -ai, -as, -a, -ons, -ez, -ont (same as avoir!).
  3. Irregular stems to memorise: aller → ir-, avoir → aur-, être → ser-, faire → fer-, pouvoir → pourr-, vouloir → voudr-, voir → verr-, savoir → saur-, venir → viendr-.

  • Si tu travailles, tu réussiras.If you work, you will succeed.
— 09 · Le conditionnel

The conditional.

Essential Politeness

Used for "would" statements, hypotheticals, and polite requests. Formed exactly like the future, but with imparfait endings.

The formula
  1. Stem: same as the future (infinitive, or irregular stem).
  2. Endings: imparfait endings — -ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient.

In context
  • Je voudrais un café, s'il vous plaît.I would like a coffee, please. (polite)
  • Si j'avais le temps, je voyagerais plus.If I had time, I would travel more. (hypothesis)
  • Le gouvernement devrait agir.The government should act. (opinion writing)
Opinion-piece gold — "Je dirais que…", "On pourrait penser que…", "Il faudrait que…" — these conditional openers consistently lift written answers. Memorise three and use them.
— 10 · Le subjonctif

The subjunctive.

Advanced After triggers

Used to express doubt, emotion, necessity, or desire — typically after specific trigger phrases like il faut que, je veux que, bien que. Show one or two of these correctly and you instantly look like a confident higher-level student.

How to form it
  1. Stem: take the ils/elles form of the present, drop the -ent. parlent → parl-
  2. Endings: -e, -es, -e, -ions, -iez, -ent.
  3. Common irregulars: avoir, être, aller, faire, pouvoir, savoir, vouloir — memorise these.

Trigger phrases — memorise five
  1. Il faut que… — it is necessary that…
  2. Je veux que… — I want… to
  3. Bien que… — although…
  4. Avant que… — before…
  5. Pour que… — so that…
In context
  • Il faut que je fasse mes devoirs.I have to do my homework.
  • Bien qu'il soit tard, je continue à étudier.Although it's late, I'm still studying.
  • Je veux que tu viennes avec moi.I want you to come with me.
