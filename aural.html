Five sections, in order.

Each paper has the same five sections in the same order. Sections A–D are played three times (full → segmented → full). Section E (news) is played twice only.

How to play it.

Four habits that move marks in this paper, taken from the chief examiner's reports.

1
Answers in English — not full sentences.
The SEC instructions are explicit: "not necessarily in complete sentences". Single phrases or bullet points score full marks. If you write in French, a 50% penalty is applied to the section.
2
First listen = gist only. Don't write.
Use the first full play to understand the topic and identify speakers. Start writing on the second (segmented) play. Final play is for verification.
3
Section E is played twice only.
Pre-read the three questions before the audio starts. News items are fast and short — you don't get the leisurely third pass that A–D give you.
4
"Two details" means two distinct points.
If the question asks for two details and you write one detail twice, you only get half marks. Each detail must be a separate idea. The marking scheme is unforgiving here.
5
Watch the numbers.
Specific quantities (money, time, age, distance) appear in every paper. An incorrect amount = 0 marks, even if everything else is right. Always cross-check on the third play.
6
Common topics: jobs, holidays, school, family.
Section B is nearly always someone describing their job. Section D is usually a current-affairs interview. Drill the vocab on the Vocab page — it'll come up.
