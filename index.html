This week's five tips

From the chief examiner's reports — small things that consistently move marks on the higher paper.

1
Anchor opinion pieces in the stimulus.
Quote or paraphrase it explicitly in your opening sentence.
2
Use three tenses, minimum.
Present + passé composé + imparfait shows control even in short pieces.
3
Learn three sophisticated connectors.
"Néanmoins, en revanche, par ailleurs" — drop them naturally.
4
Section II is half the written paper.
Budget 90 minutes for it. Most students under-allocate.
5
Final 8 minutes: verb endings and accents.
The cheapest marks on the whole paper.
